The Problem

Your QA team spends days clicking through the same regression tests every release cycle. When deadlines hit, tests get skipped. Bugs reach production. Hiring a QA automation engineer costs as much as a senior developer — and they still need weeks to ramp up.

How It Works

1

Describe the test

Type or speak: "Click New, fill the form, click Save, verify the value matches." No code. No scripts. Just English.

2

AI drives the browser

A real Chromium browser opens in the cloud. The AI reads the page, clicks buttons, fills dropdowns, handles date pickers, navigates menus — just like a human would.

3

Results in Teams

Pass or fail, step by step, delivered right to Microsoft Teams. Video replay on demand for debugging. No context switching.

4

QA fixes by rephrasing

When a test breaks, the QA describes the step differently. No developer needed. No code changes. No deployment.

Why It's Different
